Tutorial 2.3  –  Shear Force and Bending Moment Diagrams

Problem Statement

Draw the shear force and bending moment diagrams for the loaded beam and find the maximum magnitude $M$ of the bending moment.

Given answer:   $M_{\max} = \dfrac{5Pl}{6}$

Strategy: Replace the UDL with an equivalent point load to find reactions, then build $V(x)$ segment by segment (noting the UDL makes $V$ linear within $[0,l]$) and integrate to get $M(x)$ (parabolic in $[0,l]$, linear elsewhere).

Solution

1
Find the support reactions
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 1 Replace UDL with equivalent point load, then apply $\sum M_A = 0$ and $\sum F_y = 0$

The UDL of intensity $P/l$ over length $l$ is equivalent to a single downward force of magnitude $(P/l)\times l = P$, acting at the centroid of the UDL region, which is at $x = l/2$.

Taking moments about $A$ (CCW positive), with $R_B$ acting upward at $x = 3l$:

$$\sum M_A = 0:\quad R_B(3l) - P\!\left(\frac{l}{2}\right) - P(2l) = 0$$ $$R_B = \frac{\tfrac{Pl}{2} + 2Pl}{3l} = \frac{\tfrac{5Pl}{2}}{3l} = \mathbf{\frac{5P}{6} \uparrow}$$
$$\sum F_y = 0:\quad R_A + \frac{5P}{6} = P + P = 2P \implies \mathbf{R_A = \frac{7P}{6} \uparrow}$$

$R_A = \dfrac{7P}{6}\ \uparrow$     $R_B = \dfrac{5P}{6}\ \uparrow$

Check: Total upward = $7P/6 + 5P/6 = 12P/6 = 2P$ = total downward ($P + P$). ✓

2
Construct the shear force diagram $V(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 2 UDL makes $V$ linear in $[0,l]$; constant elsewhere; point load causes jump at $x=2l$

① From $A$ to $x = l$ (UDL region):

$$V(x) = R_A - \frac{P}{l}\,x = \frac{7P}{6} - \frac{P}{l}\,x$$ $$V(0) = \frac{7P}{6}, \qquad V(l) = \frac{7P}{6} - P = \frac{P}{6}$$

② From $x = l$ to $x = 2l$ (no load):

$$V = \frac{P}{6} \quad \text{(constant)}$$

③ Jump at $x = 2l$ (point load $P$ downward):

$$V(2l^+) = \frac{P}{6} - P = -\frac{5P}{6}$$

④ From $x = 2l$ to $B$ (no load):

$$V = -\frac{5P}{6} \quad \text{(constant)}$$

At $B$, $R_B = 5P/6$ restores $V$ to zero. ✓

$V$ does not pass through zero smoothly anywhere — it jumps from $+P/6$ to $-5P/6$ at $x = 2l$ via the point load discontinuity. This tells us $M_{\max}$ occurs at $x = 2l$.

3
Construct the bending moment diagram $M(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 3 Parabola in $[0,l]$, two linear segments from $l$ to $B$ — maximum at $x = 2l$

For each cut, apply $\sum M_A = 0$ (moments about $A$, CCW positive) to the left segment, reusing $V(x)$ from Step 2 — $R_A$ passes through $A$ and drops out.

① From $A$ to $x = l$ (UDL region, parabolic):

$$\sum M_A = 0: \quad M - Vx - \frac{P}{l}x\cdot\frac{x}{2} = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = Vx + \frac{P}{2l}x^2 = \frac{7P}{6}\,x - \frac{P}{2l}\,x^2$$ $$M(l) = \frac{7Pl}{6} - \frac{Pl}{2} = \frac{7Pl}{6} - \frac{3Pl}{6} = \frac{2Pl}{3}$$

② From $x = l$ to $x = 2l$ (no distributed load, linear):

$$\sum M_A = 0: \quad M - Vx - P\,\frac{l}{2} = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = Vx + \frac{Pl}{2} = \frac{P}{6}\,x + \frac{Pl}{2}$$ $$M(2l) = \frac{P}{6}(2l) + \frac{Pl}{2} = \frac{Pl}{3} + \frac{Pl}{2} = \frac{2Pl}{6} + \frac{3Pl}{6} = \boxed{\frac{5Pl}{6}} \quad \checkmark$$

③ From $x = 2l$ to $B$ (linear, slope $= -5P/6$):

$$\sum M_A = 0: \quad M - Vx - P\,\frac{l}{2} - P(2l) = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = Vx + \frac{5Pl}{2} = -\frac{5P}{6}\,x + \frac{5Pl}{2}$$ $$M(3l) = -\frac{15Pl}{6} + \frac{15Pl}{6} = 0 \quad \checkmark$$

$M_{\max} = \dfrac{5Pl}{6}$ at $x = 2l$

4
Summary
Step 4 Beam free-body diagram, shear force diagram, and bending moment diagram — aligned

$M_{\max} = \dfrac{5Pl}{6}$ at $x = 2l$

Common mistakes:

① Taking the UDL moment arm as $l$ instead of $l/2$ — the resultant of the UDL acts at its centroid, halfway along its length ($x = l/2$), not at $x = l$.

② Drawing $V(x)$ as constant in the UDL region — a UDL gives a linearly varying $V$, not a step. Only point loads and support reactions produce step changes.

③ Drawing the BMD as linear in the UDL region — since $V$ is linear there, $M$ must be parabolic ($dM/dx = V$). Forgetting this leads to an incorrect triangular BMD shape in $[0,l]$.
