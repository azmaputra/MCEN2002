Tutorial 2.5  –  Shear Force and Bending Moment Diagrams

Problem Statement

Draw the shear force and bending moment diagrams for the beam. Find the magnitude and position of the maximum bending moment and the local maximum moment underneath the distributed load.

Given answers:   $M_{\max} = 2800\ \text{N·m}$ at $x = 7\ \text{m}$;   $M_{\text{dist. load}} = 2625\ \text{N·m}$ at $x = 3.25\ \text{m}$

Key feature: A concentrated moment causes a jump in the bending moment diagram but does not affect the shear force. The shear diagram is therefore continuous at $x = 7\ \text{m}$, but the BMD has a sudden step of $\pm 4.2\ \text{kN·m}$ there.

Solution

1
Find the support reactions
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 1 Replace UDL with its resultant; include the concentrated moment in $\sum M_A = 0$

The UDL of 800 N/m over 3 m (from $x=2$ m to $x=5$ m) is equivalent to a single downward force of $800 \times 3 = 2400\ \text{N}$ acting at the centroid: $x = 2 + 1.5 = 3.5\ \text{m}$.

The concentrated moment 4.2 kN·m is clockwise (negative in our CCW-positive convention).

$$\sum M_A = 0:\quad R_B(9) - 2400(3.5) - 4200 = 0$$ $$R_B = \frac{8400 + 4200}{9} = \frac{12600}{9} = \mathbf{1400\ \text{N} \uparrow}$$
$$\sum F_y = 0:\quad R_A + 1400 = 2400 \implies \mathbf{R_A = 1000\ \text{N} \uparrow}$$

$R_A = 1000\ \text{N (upward)}$     $R_B = 1400\ \text{N (upward)}$

Check: $\sum M_B = -1000(9) + 2400(5.5) - 4200 = -9000 + 13200 - 4200 = 0$ ✓

2
Construct the shear force diagram $V(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 2 Concentrated moments do not create shear jumps — the SFD is continuous at $x = 7\ \text{m}$

① From $A$ to UDL start ($0 \le x \le 2\ \text{m}$):

$$V = +1000\ \text{N} \quad \text{(constant)}$$

② Through the UDL zone ($2\ \text{m} \le x \le 5\ \text{m}$):

$$V(x) = 1000 - 800(x - 2)$$ $$V(2) = +1000\ \text{N}, \qquad V(5) = 1000 - 2400 = -1400\ \text{N}$$

Setting $V = 0$ to find the local moment maximum under the distributed load:

$$1000 - 800(x-2) = 0 \implies x = 2 + 1.25 = \mathbf{3.25\ \text{m}} \quad \checkmark$$

③ From UDL end to $B$ ($5\ \text{m} \le x \le 9\ \text{m}$):

$$V = 1000 - 2400 = -1400\ \text{N} \quad \text{(constant — moment at $x=7$ m causes no shear jump)}$$

At $B$: $-1400 + 1400 = 0$ ✓

3
Construct the bending moment diagram $M(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 3 Integrate $V(x)$ piecewise; the BMD jumps by $+4200\ \text{N·m}$ at the clockwise applied moment

① From $A$ to UDL start ($0 \le x \le 2\ \text{m}$):

$$M(x) = 1000x \implies M(2) = 2000\ \text{N·m}$$

② Through the UDL zone ($2 \le x \le 5\ \text{m}$, parabolic):

$$M(x) = 1000x - 400(x-2)^2$$ $$M(3.25) = 1000(3.25) - 400(1.25)^2 = 3250 - 625 = \mathbf{2625\ \text{N·m}} \quad\checkmark$$ $$M(5) = 5000 - 400(9) = 1400\ \text{N·m}$$

③ From UDL end to the applied moment ($5 \le x \le 7\ \text{m}$):

$$M(x) = 1400 + (-1400)(x-5) = 1400 - 1400(x-5)$$ $$M(6) = 0,\qquad M(7^-) = 1400 - 2800 = -1400\ \text{N·m}$$

④ Jump at $x = 7\ \text{m}$ due to clockwise moment:

A clockwise applied moment causes a positive (upward) jump in the bending moment diagram:

$$M(7^+) = M(7^-) + 4200 = -1400 + 4200 = \mathbf{+2800\ \text{N·m}}$$

⑤ From applied moment to $B$ ($7 \le x \le 9\ \text{m}$):

$$M(x) = 2800 + (-1400)(x-7) \implies M(9) = 2800 - 2800 = 0 \quad\checkmark$$

$M_{\max} = 2800\ \text{N·m}$ at $x = 7\ \text{m}$   |   $M_{\text{dist. load}} = 2625\ \text{N·m}$ at $x = 3.25\ \text{m}$

4
Summary
Step 4 Beam FBD, shear force diagram, and bending moment diagram — aligned

$M_{\max} = 2800\ \text{N·m}$ at $x = 7\ \text{m}$  ·  $M_{\text{dist. load}} = 2625\ \text{N·m}$ at $x = 3.25\ \text{m}$

Common mistakes:

① Treating the concentrated moment as a force in $\sum F_y$ — a moment has no vertical component; only its effect on $\sum M_A$ matters when finding reactions.

② Adding a shear jump at $x=7$ m — concentrated moments cause a jump in the bending moment diagram only, not in the shear force diagram.

③ Wrong sign for the BMD jump — a clockwise applied moment causes a positive (upward) jump in BMD; a counterclockwise applied moment causes a negative jump. Always verify using the right-hand segment equilibrium or by checking boundary conditions at both supports.
