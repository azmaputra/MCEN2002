Tutorial 2.6  –  Shear Force and Bending Moment Diagrams

Problem Statement

Draw the shear force and bending moment diagrams for the beam and find the magnitude and position of the maximum bending moment.

Given answer:   $M_{\max} = 4.5\ \text{kN·m}$ at $x = 1\ \text{m}$

Overhanging beam: This beam has free ends that overhang past each support. The overhanging portions act like short cantilevers, creating hogging (negative) bending moments at the roller supports. The maximum sagging (positive) moment occurs at midspan. Expect the BMD to dip negative at the supports.

Solution

1
Find the support reactions
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 1 Use symmetry — the geometry and loading are symmetric about midspan $x = 1$ m

The total UDL resultant is $15 \times 2 = 30\ \text{kN}$ downward. By symmetry of both the loading and the support positions:

$$R_L = R_R = \frac{30}{2} = \mathbf{15\ \text{kN} \uparrow}$$

Verify with $\sum M$: Taking moments about the left roller at $x=0.2$ m: $15 \times 2 \times (1 - 0.2) - R_R \times 1.6 = 30(0.8) - 15(1.6) = 24 - 24 = 0$ ✓

2
Construct the shear force diagram $V(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 2 UDL across the full span — $V$ is linear throughout, with jumps at each roller

① Left overhang ($0 \le x \le 0.2\ \text{m}$):

$$V(x) = -15x$$ $$V(0) = 0,\qquad V(0.2^-) = -3\ \text{kN}$$

② Jump at left roller ($x = 0.2\ \text{m}$):

$$V(0.2^+) = -3 + 15 = +12\ \text{kN}$$

③ Between rollers ($0.2 \le x \le 1.8\ \text{m}$):

$$V(x) = -3 + 15 - 15(x - 0.2) = 12 - 15(x - 0.2)$$ $$V(0.2^+) = +12\ \text{kN},\qquad V(1.8^-) = 12 - 15(1.6) = -12\ \text{kN}$$

By symmetry, $V = 0$ at midspan:

$$12 - 15(x - 0.2) = 0 \implies x = 0.2 + 0.8 = \mathbf{1.0\ \text{m}} \quad\checkmark$$

④ Jump at right roller, then right overhang ($1.8 \le x \le 2\ \text{m}$):

$$V(1.8^+) = -12 + 15 = +3\ \text{kN}$$ $$V(x) = 3 - 15(x-1.8),\qquad V(2) = 3 - 3 = 0 \quad\checkmark$$
3
Construct the bending moment diagram $M(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 3 UDL throughout gives a parabolic BMD — negative (hogging) at the rollers, positive (sagging) at midspan

For each cut, apply $\sum M_O = 0$ — moments about the left free end $O$ ($x=0$), CCW positive — to the left segment, reusing $V(x)$ from Step 2. (There is no pin here, so the fixed reference point is the beam's left end.)

① Left overhang ($0 \le x \le 0.2\ \text{m}$):

$$\sum M_O = 0: \quad M - Vx - 15x\cdot\frac{x}{2} = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = Vx + 7.5x^2 = -7.5x^2$$ $$M(0) = 0,\qquad M(0.2) = -7.5(0.04) = -0.3\ \text{kN·m}$$

② Between rollers ($0.2 \le x \le 1.8\ \text{m}$):

$$\sum M_O = 0: \quad M - Vx - 15x\cdot\frac{x}{2} + 15(0.2) = 0$$ $$\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = Vx + 7.5x^2 - 3 = -7.5x^2 + 15x - 3$$

At midspan ($x = 1.0$ m, where $V = 0$):

$$M(1.0) = -7.5(1) + 15(1) - 3 = -7.5 + 15 - 3 = \mathbf{4.5\ \text{kN·m}} \quad\checkmark$$

At right roller ($x = 1.8$ m), by symmetry:

$$M(1.8) = -0.3\ \text{kN·m} \quad\text{(symmetric to left roller)}$$

③ Right overhang ($1.8 \le x \le 2\ \text{m}$):

$$\sum M_O = 0: \quad M - Vx - 15x\cdot\frac{x}{2} + 15(0.2) + 15(1.8) = 0$$ $$\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = Vx + 7.5x^2 - 30 = -7.5(2-x)^2, \qquad M(2) = 0 \quad\checkmark$$

$M_{\max} = 4.5\ \text{kN·m}$ at $x = 1.0\ \text{m}$ (midspan)

4
Summary
Step 4 Beam FBD, shear force diagram, and bending moment diagram — aligned

$M_{\max} = 4.5\ \text{kN·m}$ at $x = 1.0\ \text{m}$ (midspan)

Common mistakes:

① Placing supports at the beam ends — the rollers are at $x=0.2$ m and $x=1.8$ m, not at the ends. The 0.2 m overhangs are free, so $M=0$ at both ends, but $M \ne 0$ at the support positions.

② Expecting only positive moments — the overhanging portions act like short cantilevers and produce hogging ($-$ve) moments just inside the supports. Always check $M$ at the support locations when overhangs are present.

③ Drawing the BMD as piecewise linear — the UDL extends over the full 2 m span, so the entire BMD is parabolic. There are no straight-line segments anywhere on this diagram.
