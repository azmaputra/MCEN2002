Tutorial 2.2  –  Shear Force and Bending Moment Diagrams

Problem Statement

The angle strut is welded to the end $C$ of the I-beam and supports the 1.6 kN vertical force. Determine the bending moment at $B$ and the distance $x$ to the left of $C$ at which the bending moment is zero. Also construct the shear force and bending moment diagrams for the beam.

Given answer:   $M_B = -0.4\ \text{kNm}$     $x = 0.2\ \text{m}$

Key idea: The strut is not a simple point load — it is eccentric. The 1.6 kN force acts 200 mm away from $C$, so the strut transmits an equivalent force + couple to the beam at $C$. Replace these before applying equilibrium.

Solution

1
Equivalent loading at $C$ and support reactions
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 1 Replace the strut with equivalent loads at $C$, then apply $\sum M_A = 0$ and $\sum F_y = 0$

① Equivalent loading at $C$

The 1.6 kN force acts at the tip of the strut, which is 200 mm to the left of $C$. Moving this force to $C$ requires adding the equivalent couple:

$$M_C = 1.6 \times 0.2 = 0.32\ \text{kNm} \quad \text{(counterclockwise)}$$

So the beam at $C$ sees: a 1.6 kN downward force + a 0.32 kNm CCW moment.

② Sum moments about $A$ (CCW positive)

$$\sum M_A = 0:\quad R_B(0.4) - 1.6(0.85) + 0.32 = 0$$ $$R_B = \frac{1.6(0.85) - 0.32}{0.4} = \frac{1.04}{0.4} = \mathbf{2.6\ \text{kN} \uparrow}$$

③ Sum vertical forces

$$\sum F_y = 0:\quad R_A + 2.6 - 1.6 = 0 \implies R_A = -1.0\ \text{kN}$$

$R_B = 2.6\ \text{kN (upward)}$     $R_A = 1.0\ \text{kN \textbf{downward}}$

Why is $R_A$ downward? The roller at $B$ provides a very large upward reaction (2.6 kN) to counteract both the 1.6 kN load and the applied couple — it overshoots vertical equilibrium, so the pin at $A$ must pull the beam down with 1.0 kN. Pins resist force in any direction, so this is physically valid.

2
Construct the shear force diagram $V(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 2 Track $V(x)$ from left to right — $R_A$ is downward, so $V$ starts negative

Moving left to right and accumulating vertical forces (upward = positive shear contribution):

① From $A$ to $B$ ($0 \le x < 0.4\ \text{m}$):

$$V(x) = R_A = -1.0\ \text{kN}$$

② From $B$ to $C$ ($0.4\ \text{m} < x \le 0.85\ \text{m}$):

$$V(x) = R_A + R_B = -1.0 + 2.6 = +1.6\ \text{kN}$$

At $C$, the 1.6 kN downward force brings $V$ back to zero. The couple at $C$ does not affect the shear diagram.

3
Construct the bending moment diagram $M(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 3 Integrate $V(x)$, identify $M_B$, locate the zero crossing, and apply the couple jump at $C$

Since $dM/dx = V$, $M(x)$ is linear on each segment with slope equal to the local shear. $M = 0$ at the pin $A$ (simple support).

① From $A$ to $B$ ($0 \le x \le 0.4\ \text{m}$):

$$M(x) = R_A\, x = -1.0\, x\ \text{kNm}$$ $$\boxed{M_B = M(0.4) = -0.4\ \text{kNm}} \quad \checkmark$$

② From $B$ to $C$ ($0.4\ \text{m} \le x \le 0.85\ \text{m}$):

$$M(x) = -1.0\,x + 2.6(x - 0.4) = 1.6x - 1.04\ \text{kNm}$$

③ Distance $x$ to the left of $C$ where $M = 0$:

$$1.6x - 1.04 = 0 \implies x = \frac{1.04}{1.6} = 0.65\ \text{m from } A$$ $$\boxed{x = 0.85 - 0.65 = 0.2\ \text{m from } C} \quad \checkmark$$

④ Value just before $C$, and the couple jump:

$$M(0.85^-) = 1.6(0.85) - 1.04 = 0.32\ \text{kNm}$$

The 0.32 kNm CCW couple at $C$ drops $M$ by 0.32 kNm, returning to zero at the free end — as required.

$$M(0.85^+) = 0.32 - 0.32 = 0\ \quad \checkmark$$

$M_B = -0.4\ \text{kNm}$     Zero crossing: $x = 0.2\ \text{m}$ to the left of $C$

4
Summary
Step 4 Beam free-body diagram, shear force diagram, and bending moment diagram — aligned

$M_B = -0.4\ \text{kNm}$     Zero-moment location: $x = 0.2\ \text{m}$ to the left of $C$

Common mistakes:

① Ignoring the eccentric couple at $C$ — treating the strut as a simple 1.6 kN point load at $C$ omits the 0.32 kNm couple, giving wrong reactions and wrong diagrams.

② Assuming $R_A$ is upward — when the algebra gives a negative reaction, it acts in the opposite direction to your assumed sense. Always re-draw the arrow correctly before constructing the SFD.

③ Forgetting the moment jump at $C$ in the BMD — the 0.32 kNm couple causes a discontinuous drop in the bending moment at $C$, closing the diagram to zero. Missing this jump leaves $M(C) = +0.32\ \text{kNm}$, not zero.
