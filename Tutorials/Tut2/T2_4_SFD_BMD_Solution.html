Tutorial 2.4  –  Shear Force and Bending Moment Diagrams

Problem Statement

Draw the shear force and bending moment diagrams for the beam and find the magnitude and position of the maximum bending moment.

Given answer:   $M_{\max} = 5625\ \text{N·m}$ at $x = 4.25\ \text{m}$

Strategy: Find reactions, then build $V(x)$ segment by segment (linear in the UDL zone). Locate the zero of $V$ within the UDL zone — that is where $M$ is maximum. Integrate to get $M(x)$ (parabolic in the UDL zone, linear everywhere else).

Solution

1
Find the support reactions
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 1 Replace UDL with its resultant, then apply $\sum M_A = 0$ and $\sum F_y = 0$

The UDL of 800 N/m over 3 m (from $x=2$ m to $x=5$ m) is equivalent to a single downward force of magnitude $800 \times 3 = 2400$ N acting at the centroid: $x = 2 + 1.5 = 3.5$ m from $A$.

$$\sum M_A = 0:\quad R_B(9) - 2400(3.5) - 1500(7) = 0$$ $$R_B = \frac{8400 + 10500}{9} = \frac{18900}{9} = \mathbf{2100\ \text{N} \uparrow}$$
$$\sum F_y = 0:\quad R_A + 2100 = 2400 + 1500 = 3900 \implies \mathbf{R_A = 1800\ \text{N} \uparrow}$$

$R_A = 1800\ \text{N (upward)}$     $R_B = 2100\ \text{N (upward)}$

Check: $1800 + 2100 = 3900\ \text{N}$ = $2400 + 1500\ \text{N}$ ✓

2
Construct the shear force diagram $V(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 2 Constant on $[0,2]$, linear in the UDL zone $[2,5]$, two more constant segments to $B$

① From $A$ to UDL start ($0 \le x \le 2\ \text{m}$):

$$V = R_A = +1800\ \text{N} \quad \text{(constant)}$$

② Through the UDL zone ($2\ \text{m} \le x \le 5\ \text{m}$):

$$V(x) = 1800 - 800(x-2)$$ $$V(2) = +1800\ \text{N}, \qquad V(5) = 1800 - 2400 = -600\ \text{N}$$

Setting $V = 0$ to locate the maximum moment:

$$1800 - 800(x-2) = 0 \implies x - 2 = 2.25 \implies \boxed{x = 4.25\ \text{m}} \quad \checkmark$$

③ From UDL end to point load ($5\ \text{m} \le x \le 7\ \text{m}$):

$$V = 1800 - 2400 = -600\ \text{N} \quad \text{(constant)}$$

④ Jump at $x = 7\ \text{m}$ and final segment ($7\ \text{m} \le x \le 9\ \text{m}$):

$$V = -600 - 1500 = -2100\ \text{N} \quad \text{(constant)}$$

At $B$: $-2100 + 2100 = 0$ ✓

3
Construct the bending moment diagram $M(x)$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 3 Integrate $V(x)$ — parabolic in the UDL zone, linear everywhere else

For each cut, apply $\sum M_A = 0$ (moments about $A$, CCW positive) to the left segment, reusing $V(x)$ from Step 2 — $R_A$ passes through $A$ and drops out.

① From $A$ to UDL start ($0 \le x \le 2\ \text{m}$):

$$\sum M_A = 0: \quad M - Vx = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = Vx = 1800\,x \implies M(2) = 3600\ \text{N·m}$$

② Through the UDL zone ($2\ \text{m} \le x \le 5\ \text{m}$, parabolic):

$$\sum M_A = 0: \quad M - Vx - 800(x-2)\cdot\frac{x+2}{2} = 0$$ $$\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = 1800x - 800\cdot\frac{(x-2)^2}{2} = 1800x - 400(x-2)^2$$

At the zero-shear point ($x = 4.25$ m):

$$M(4.25) = 1800(4.25) - 400(2.25)^2 = 7650 - 2025 = \boxed{5625\ \text{N·m}} \quad \checkmark$$
$$M(5) = 1800(5) - 400(3)^2 = 9000 - 3600 = 5400\ \text{N·m}$$

③ From UDL end to point load ($5\ \text{m} \le x \le 7\ \text{m}$):

$$\sum M_A = 0: \quad M - Vx - 2400(3.5) = 0 \quad\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = Vx + 8400 = -600x + 8400$$ $$M(7) = -4200 + 8400 = 4200\ \text{N·m}$$

④ From point load to $B$ ($7\ \text{m} \le x \le 9\ \text{m}$):

$$\sum M_A = 0: \quad M - Vx - 2400(3.5) - 1500(7) = 0$$ $$\Rightarrow\quad M(x) = Vx + 18900 = -2100x + 18900 \implies M(9) = 0 \quad \checkmark$$

$M_{\max} = 5625\ \text{N·m}$ at $x = 4.25\ \text{m}$ from $A$

4
Summary
Step 4 Beam free-body diagram, shear force diagram, and bending moment diagram — aligned

$M_{\max} = 5625\ \text{N·m}$ at $x = 4.25\ \text{m}$ from $A$

Common mistakes:

① Taking the UDL moment arm from $A$ instead of its centroid — the resultant 2400 N acts at $x = 3.5$ m (centroid of [2,5] m), not at $x = 2$ m or $x = 5$ m.

② Missing the location of $M_{\max}$ — the maximum bending moment occurs where $V = 0$ (or changes sign), not at a support or at the point load. Setting $V(x) = 0$ within the UDL zone is the key step.

③ Drawing the BMD as linear through the UDL zone — since $V$ is linear in [2, 5] m, $M$ must be quadratic (parabolic) in that segment. A straight line from 3600 N·m to 5400 N·m would miss the 5625 N·m peak.
