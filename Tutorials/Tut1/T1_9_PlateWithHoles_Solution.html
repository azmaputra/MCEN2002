Tutorial 1.9  –  Rectangular Plate with Three Circular Holes

Problem Statement

Determine $I_x$, $I_y$ and $I_{xy}$ for the rectangular plate with three identical circular holes.

Given answer:   $I_x = 244 \times 10^6\ \text{mm}^4$    $I_y = 980 \times 10^6\ \text{mm}^4$    $I_{xy} = -14.14 \times 10^6\ \text{mm}^4$

Solution

1
Set up the composite: solid plate minus three holes
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 1 Treat the holes as negative areas

The section is a solid rectangle with material removed. Compute each property for the full plate, then subtract the contribution of each hole (treated as a full circle, including its own parallel-axis transfer term):

$$I = I_{plate} - \sum_{i=1}^{3} \left( \bar{I}_{hole} + A_{hole}\,d_i^{\,2} \right)$$

Properties common to all three holes ($r = 30$ mm):

Hole area:   $A = \pi r^2 = \pi (30)^2 = 2\,827\text{ mm}^2$

Hole centroidal moment (any axis through its centre):   $\bar{I} = \dfrac{\pi r^4}{4} = \dfrac{\pi (30)^4}{4} = 0.636 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$

Hole centres:   $H_1(-100,\,50)$,   $H_2(100,\,50)$,   $H_3(-100,\,-50)$

The plate is centred on the x–y axes, so its own centroidal formulas apply directly with no transfer term. Note that all three holes sit at $|\bar{y}| = 50$ mm and $|\bar{x}| = 100$ mm — their $I_x$ and $I_y$ contributions are identical; only the signs matter for $I_{xy}$.

2
Calculate $I_x$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 2 Plate minus three identical hole contributions about the x-axis

Solid plate ($b = 400$, $h = 200$, centroid on the x-axis):

$$I_x^{plate} = \frac{b h^3}{12} = \frac{400 \times 200^3}{12} = 266.67 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

Each hole (every centre is $d_y = 50$ mm from the x-axis):

$$I_x^{hole} = \frac{\pi r^4}{4} + A\,d_y^2 = 0.636 \times 10^6 + 2\,827 \times 50^2 = 7.705 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

Composite:

$$I_x = 266.67 \times 10^6 - 3 \times 7.705 \times 10^6 = 243.6 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

$$I_x = 244 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

Note: the holes remove only about 9% of $I_x$ even though they remove material — because they sit relatively close to the x-axis ($d_y = 50$ mm out of a 100 mm half-depth), where material contributes least.

3
Calculate $I_y$
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 3 Plate minus three identical hole contributions about the y-axis

Solid plate:

$$I_y^{plate} = \frac{h b^3}{12} = \frac{200 \times 400^3}{12} = 1\,066.67 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

Each hole (every centre is $d_x = 100$ mm from the y-axis):

$$I_y^{hole} = \frac{\pi r^4}{4} + A\,d_x^2 = 0.636 \times 10^6 + 2\,827 \times 100^2 = 28.91 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

Composite:

$$I_y = 1\,066.67 \times 10^6 - 3 \times 28.91 \times 10^6 = 979.9 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

$$I_y = 980 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

4
Calculate $I_{xy}$ — watch the signs
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 4 Only the unpaired hole contributes

The plate is symmetric about both axes, so $I_{xy}^{plate} = 0$. Each circular hole is symmetric about its own centroidal axes, so its $\bar{I}_{xy} = 0$ and only the transfer term $A\bar{x}\bar{y}$ remains. Subtracting the holes:

$$I_{xy} = 0 - \sum_{i=1}^{3} A\,\bar{x}_i\,\bar{y}_i$$

Evaluate each hole ($A\times 100 \times 50 = 2\,827 \times 5\,000 = 14.137 \times 10^6$ mm⁴, with quadrant signs):

$$ \begin{aligned} H_1(-100,\,50):&\quad A\bar{x}\bar{y} = -14.137 \times 10^6 &&\text{(2nd quadrant, −)}\\ H_2(100,\,50):&\quad A\bar{x}\bar{y} = +14.137 \times 10^6 &&\text{(1st quadrant, +)}\\ H_3(-100,\,-50):&\quad A\bar{x}\bar{y} = +14.137 \times 10^6 &&\text{(3rd quadrant, +)} \end{aligned}$$

$H_1$ and $H_2$ are mirror images across the y-axis, so their terms cancel. Only $H_3$ survives:

$$I_{xy} = -\left( -14.137 + 14.137 + 14.137 \right) \times 10^6 = -14.137 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

$$I_{xy} = -14.14 \times 10^6\text{ mm}^4$$

Physical insight: if the plate had a fourth hole at $(100, -50)$, the pattern would be symmetric and $I_{xy}$ would vanish. The non-zero $I_{xy}$ comes entirely from the missing symmetry partner of $H_3$ — removing material in the 3rd quadrant (a positive $A\bar{x}\bar{y}$ region) drives the composite $I_{xy}$ negative.

Hole rows show contributions to the composite (hole values with sign reversed, since material is removed).

Common mistake 1: Subtracting only $A d^2$ for each hole and forgetting the hole's own centroidal term $\pi r^4/4$. It is small here (0.64 vs 7.07 ×10⁶ for $I_x$) but not negligible, and omitting it is conceptually wrong — the removed material had both terms.

Common mistake 2: For $I_{xy}$, a double sign error: each hole's transfer term carries the quadrant sign of its centre and a minus sign because the area is removed. Track both — here two of the three hole terms cancel each other, and the answer's sign comes from the lone lower-left hole.
