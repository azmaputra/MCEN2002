Tutorial 1.2  –  Centroid of a Composite Area

Problem Statement

Find the centroidal coordinates $\bar{x}$ and $\bar{y}$ of the composite shape shown below. The shape is a channel section (U-channel) with a circular hole in the right arm. All dimensions in millimetres (mm).

Given answer:   $\bar{x} = 217.1\ \text{mm}$    $\bar{y} = 198.5\ \text{mm}$

Composite Area Method

$$\bar{x} = \frac{\sum A_i\,\bar{x}_i}{\sum A_i} \qquad \bar{y} = \frac{\sum A_i\,\bar{y}_i}{\sum A_i}$$

Voids (holes and cutouts) are treated as negative areas. Two methods are shown below — both yield the same result.

Method 1 Decompose into Three Rectangles and a Circular Hole
Fig. M1 — Four-part decomposition

Split the U-channel into three solid rectangles, then subtract the circular hole:

  • ① Left arm — $150 \times 300\ \text{mm}$
  • ② Base — $450 \times 150\ \text{mm}$ (full-width bottom strip)
  • ③ Right arm — $150 \times 300\ \text{mm}$
  • ④ Hole — circle $r = 50\ \text{mm}$  (subtract)

The dashed lines show where the base ② meets the two arms.

1
Locate the Centroid of Each Part
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 1 Show solution

Origin O is at the bottom-left corner. The void floor (bottom of the opening) is at $y = 150\ \text{mm}$, so the arms each span $300\ \text{mm}$ above it.

① Left arm — $150 \times 300\ \text{mm}$, spanning $x = 0 \to 150$, $y = 150 \to 450$:

$$\bar{x}_1 = \dfrac{0+150}{2} = 75\ \text{mm} \qquad \bar{y}_1 = \dfrac{150+450}{2} = 300\ \text{mm}$$

② Base — $450 \times 150\ \text{mm}$, spanning $x = 0 \to 450$, $y = 0 \to 150$:

$$\bar{x}_2 = \dfrac{0+450}{2} = 225\ \text{mm} \qquad \bar{y}_2 = \dfrac{0+150}{2} = 75\ \text{mm}$$

③ Right arm — $150 \times 300\ \text{mm}$, spanning $x = 300 \to 450$, $y = 150 \to 450$:

$$\bar{x}_3 = \dfrac{300+450}{2} = 375\ \text{mm} \qquad \bar{y}_3 = \dfrac{150+450}{2} = 300\ \text{mm}$$

④ Circular hole — $r = 50\ \text{mm}$, centre located $75\ \text{mm}$ from the inner face of the right arm at $x = 300$, and midway between the void floor and the arm top:

$$\bar{x}_4 = 300 + 75 = 375\ \text{mm} \qquad \bar{y}_4 = 150 + 150 = 300\ \text{mm}$$

Method 2 Full Rectangle Minus Rectangular Void Minus Circular Hole
Fig. M2 — Subtraction approach

Start with a complete bounding rectangle and subtract two voids:

  • ① Full rectangle — $450 \times 450\ \text{mm}$  (add)
  • ② Rectangular void — $150 \times 300\ \text{mm}$, the gap between the arms  (subtract)
  • ③ Circular hole — $r = 50\ \text{mm}$  (subtract)

Red regions are subtracted. The dashed outline shows the full $450\times450$ bounding rectangle.

Step 1 Show solution

① Full rectangle — $450 \times 450\ \text{mm}$, spanning $x = 0 \to 450$, $y = 0 \to 450$:

$$\bar{x}_1 = \frac{450}{2} = 225\ \text{mm} \qquad \bar{y}_1 = \frac{450}{2} = 225\ \text{mm}$$

② Rectangular void — $150 \times 300\ \text{mm}$, spanning $x = 150 \to 300$, $y = 150 \to 450$ (the gap between the arms):

$$\bar{x}_2 = 150 + \frac{150}{2} = 225\ \text{mm} \qquad \bar{y}_2 = 150 + \frac{300}{2} = 300\ \text{mm}$$

③ Circular hole — $r = 50\ \text{mm}$, centre $75\ \text{mm}$ from inner face of right arm at $x = 300$, midway between void floor and arm top:

$$\bar{x}_3 = 300 + 75 = 375\ \text{mm} \qquad \bar{y}_3 = 150 + 150 = 300\ \text{mm}$$

Both methods yield the same result:

$\bar{x} = 217.1\ \text{mm}$     $\bar{y} = 198.5\ \text{mm}$

The centroid lies below and to the left of the geometric centre $(225,\ 225)\ \text{mm}$, because the circular hole removes material from the upper-right area of the shape.
