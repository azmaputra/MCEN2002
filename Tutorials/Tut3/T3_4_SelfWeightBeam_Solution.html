Tutorial 3.4  –  Bending Stresses due to Self-Weight (Overhanging Beam)

Problem Statement

Calculate the maximum tensile and compressive stress due to the self-weight of the beam shown. Material density $\rho = 7\,800$ kg/m³. The beam has a pin at A, a roller at B (3 m from A), and a 1 m overhang beyond B. The cross-section is a symmetric trapezoid: bottom width 16 cm, sides of length 8 cm inclined at 30° to the vertical.

Given answer:   $\sigma_c = 6.79\ \text{MPa}$,   $\sigma_t = 5.43\ \text{MPa}$

Solution

1
Resolve the trapezoid geometry from the 30° and 8 cm labels
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 1 The 8 cm is the slant side, not the height

The 8 cm dimension is marked along the inclined side, and the 30° angle is measured between that side and the vertical. Resolving:

$$h = 8\cos 30° = 6.928\ \text{cm} = 69.28\ \text{mm}$$
$$\text{horizontal offset per side} = 8\sin 30° = 4\ \text{cm} \;\Rightarrow\; \text{top width } a = 16 - 2(4) = 8\ \text{cm} = 80\ \text{mm}$$

Cross-sectional area:

$$A = \frac{(a + b)}{2}\,h = \frac{(80 + 160)}{2} \times 69.28 = 8\,314\ \text{mm}^2 = 8.314 \times 10^{-3}\ \text{m}^2$$

Why this reading: treating 8 cm as the vertical height instead gives $\sigma_c \approx 5.9$ MPa — inconsistent with the book answer. The slant-side reading reproduces 6.79/5.43 MPa exactly (Step 5), which confirms the geometry.

2
Self-weight as a uniformly distributed load
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 2 $w = \rho A g$ per metre of beam

Each metre of beam has volume $A \times 1$ m, so the weight per unit length is:

$$w = \rho A g = 7\,800 \times 8.314 \times 10^{-3} \times 9.81$$

$$w = 636.2\ \text{N/m}$$

Sanity check: the whole 4 m beam weighs $636.2 \times 4 = 2\,545$ N ≈ 260 kg — reasonable for a 4 m steel member with an 83 cm² section. ✓

3
Reactions and bending moment diagram
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 3 Two critical sections: max sagging in the span, max hogging at B

Total load $4w$ acts at the beam's mid-length, 2 m from A. Taking moments about A ($L_{AB} = 3$ m):

$$R_B(3) = 4w(2) \;\Rightarrow\; R_B = \frac{8w}{3} = 2.667w \qquad R_A = 4w - R_B = 1.333w$$

With $w = 636.2$ N/m: $R_A = 848.2$ N and $R_B = 1\,696.4$ N.

Cut 1: section at $x$ between A and B  ($0 \le x \le 3$ m)

Cut the beam at $x$ and keep the left free body: it carries $R_A$ up, the weight $wx$ down, and the internal $V$ and $M$ exposed at the cut face (drawn in their positive sign convention):

$$\sum F_y = 0:\quad R_A - V(x) - wx = 0$$ $$V(x) = R_A - wx = w(1.333 - x)$$
$$\sum M_A = 0:\quad M(x) - V(x)\,x - \frac{wx^2}{2} = 0$$ $$M(x) = V(x)\,x + \frac{wx^2}{2} = R_A\,x - \frac{wx^2}{2} = w\left(1.333\,x - \frac{x^2}{2}\right)$$

The shear crosses zero at $x = R_A/w = 1.333$ m — the sagging maximum:

$$M_{\text{sag}} = w\left(1.333 \times 1.333 - \frac{1.333^2}{2}\right) = 0.889w = 565.5\ \text{N}\!\cdot\!\text{m}$$

At $x = 3^-$ (just left of B): $V = w(1.333 - 3) = -1.667w = -1\,060.3$ N and $M = w(4 - 4.5) = -0.5w = -318.1$ N·m.

Cut 2: section in the overhang  ($3 \le x \le 4$ m)

Here it is quicker to keep the right free body — only the weight of the remaining stub $\xi = 4 - x$ acts on it, no reactions:

$$V(x) = +\,w\,(4 - x) \qquad M(x) = -\,\frac{w\,(4 - x)^2}{2}$$

At B ($x = 3$): $V = +w = +636.2$ N and $M = -0.5w = -318.1$ N·m — matching Cut 1's moment (it must: $M$ is continuous), while the shear jumps by $R_B = 2.667w$ across the roller. At the free end both vanish. ✓

$$M_B = -\frac{w(1)^2}{2} = -0.5w = -318.1\ \text{N}\!\cdot\!\text{m} \;\text{(hogging: top tension)}$$

SFD and BMD

Both sections must be checked. The sagging and hogging moments load opposite fibres in tension. Here $|M_{\text{sag}}| = 0.889w > |M_B| = 0.5w$, but with an asymmetric section the smaller moment could still govern one of the two stresses — never skip the comparison (Step 5).

4
Section properties of the trapezoid
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 4 Centroid and $I$ from the trapezoid formulas

With $a = 80$ mm (top), $b = 160$ mm (bottom), $h = 69.28$ mm, measuring from the bottom:

$$\bar{y} = \frac{h}{3}\,\frac{2a + b}{a + b} = \frac{69.28}{3} \times \frac{160 + 160}{240} = 30.79\ \text{mm}$$
$$y_1 = 30.79\ \text{mm (bottom fibre)} \qquad y_2 = 69.28 - 30.79 = 38.49\ \text{mm (top fibre)}$$
$$I = \frac{h^3(a^2 + 4ab + b^2)}{36(a + b)} = \frac{69.28^3\,(6\,400 + 51\,200 + 25\,600)}{36(240)}$$

$$I = 3.202 \times 10^6\ \text{mm}^4 \;(= 320.2\ \text{cm}^4), \qquad \bar{y} = 30.79\ \text{mm}$$

Physical check: the section is wider at the bottom, so the centroid sits below mid-height ($h/2 = 34.6$ mm) — 30.79 mm is consistent. ✓

5
Stresses at both critical sections
Try it first, then reveal ↓
Step 5 Four candidate stresses — the sagging section wins both

Sagging section ($M = 565.5$ N·m = $565.5 \times 10^3$ N·mm): bottom in tension, top in compression:

$$\sigma_c^{\text{(top)}} = \frac{565.5 \times 10^3 \times 38.49}{3.202 \times 10^6} = 6.80\ \text{MPa} \qquad \sigma_t^{\text{(bot)}} = \frac{565.5 \times 10^3 \times 30.79}{3.202 \times 10^6} = 5.44\ \text{MPa}$$

Hogging section at B ($M = 318.1$ N·m): top in tension, bottom in compression:

$$\sigma_t^{\text{(top)}} = \frac{318.1 \times 10^3 \times 38.49}{3.202 \times 10^6} = 3.82\ \text{MPa} \qquad \sigma_c^{\text{(bot)}} = \frac{318.1 \times 10^3 \times 30.79}{3.202 \times 10^6} = 3.06\ \text{MPa}$$

The sagging section governs both maxima:

$$\sigma_c = 6.79\ \text{MPa (top fibre, } x = 1.33\ \text{m from A)} \qquad \sigma_t = 5.43\ \text{MPa (bottom fibre, same section)}$$

Note on rounding: carrying full precision gives 6.80 and 5.44 MPa; the book's 6.79/5.43 comes from rounding $w$, $M$ or $I$ at intermediate steps (<0.2% difference). Either is acceptable.

Common mistake 1: Reading the 8 cm as the vertical height of the trapezoid. It is the slant side; the height is $8\cos 30° = 6.93$ cm and the top width is 8 cm. Using $h = 8$ cm changes $A$, $w$, $\bar{y}$ and $I$ simultaneously and misses the book answer.

Common mistake 2: Treating the beam as simply supported over 4 m and using $M = wL^2/8 = 2w$. The roller is at 3 m with a 1 m overhang — the correct sagging maximum is $0.889w$ at $x = 1.333$ m (where the shear crosses zero, not at mid-span), plus a hogging moment $-0.5w$ at B.

Common mistake 3: Checking only the largest moment. With an asymmetric section, hogging at B puts the long fibre ($y_2$) in tension, so it could govern $\sigma_t$ even with a smaller $|M|$. Here it doesn't ($3.82 < 5.43$ MPa), but only the explicit comparison in Step 5 proves that.

Common mistake 4: Forgetting $g$ — using $\rho A$ (kg/m) directly as a force per metre. Density gives mass; multiply by $g = 9.81$ m/s² to get the weight intensity in N/m.
